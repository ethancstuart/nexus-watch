# X Threads — Good Examples

Five exemplary daily-brief-style X threads. Each thread is numbered tweet by
tweet. Every individual tweet must stand alone if someone quote-tweets it.

---

## 1. Daily Brief: Hormuz & Red Sea

**1/5** ☕ Five things caught our eye on the maps this morning.

**2/5** The Strait of Hormuz chokepoint status flipped from "normal" to "elevated" overnight. Our AIS layer is showing two tankers holding just east of the strait with AIS on but engines idle. Neither has filed a new destination in 6+ hours.

**3/5** Red Sea: 18 vessels rerouted around Cape of Good Hope this week, down from a Q1 2024 peak of 60+. Quieter than headlines suggest, but not back to baseline.

**4/5** On the cyber layer: a cluster of DDoS reports against Saudi banking infrastructure yesterday. Attribution unclear. Worth watching because the last cluster like this preceded a physical incident by about 72 hours — could be coincidence.

**5/5** Tension index for the Gulf region: 68/100, up 4 points week-over-week. Full dashboard is live at nexuswatch.dev. We'll update this thread if anything in the chokepoint status changes.

**Why it works:** Single ☕ emoji in the opener, no emoji in the body. Every tweet has a number or a place name. The last tweet is the only tweet that mentions the product, and it doesn't pitch.

## 2. Daily Brief: Portugal Fires

**1/4** 🌍 Europe's fire season started early this year. Here's what our NASA FIRMS layer picked up overnight.

**2/4** 14 active wildfires along the Portuguese coast, with three inside evacuation zones near Leiria. We counted 9 active at this point in 2024 and 6 in 2023 — so this is a real jump, not a seasonal curve shift.

**3/4** The fire nearest Lisbon is 30km out and holding. Wind direction from our weather layer says it won't reach urban infrastructure in the next 24 hours unless conditions change, but the wind forecast for tomorrow afternoon is less favorable.

**4/4** Nothing from the GDACS disaster layer triggered an alert tier yet — these are below the international-coordination threshold — but local resources are visibly stretched. We'll post again if that changes.

**Why it works:** Opens with 🌍 (brand set), gives a clean year-over-year comparison, uses multiple layers (FIRMS, weather, GDACS) to triangulate, ends with a concrete "we'll post if X" commitment.

## 3. Daily Brief: Taiwan Strait Activity

**1/4** 🗺️ Taiwan Strait activity today, straight from our layers — no commentary, just the data.

**2/4** 24 PLA air incursions across the median line in the last 24 hours (above the 30-day average of 18). Mostly J-16s and one KJ-500 AEW. Our GDELT news layer shows this is the fourth consecutive day of elevated incursion counts.

**3/4** Shipping: the lane is normal. AIS traffic through the strait is within its 30-day range. Commercial maritime isn't reacting.

**4/4** Tension index for the strait: 71/100. Elevated, not breaking. Below the August 2024 peak of 84. The air layer is the interesting one this week.

**Why it works:** Opens with a promise ("no commentary, just the data") and keeps the promise. Uses numbers, names specific aircraft types, triangulates across layers. Lands on a concrete read without overclaiming.

## 4. Daily Brief: Global Disaster Roundup

**1/5** 🌍 Disaster layer roundup for the week. Five things we were watching.

**2/5** Türkiye: M5.8 northeast of Malatya early Tuesday. Shallow (14km). No structural damage reported, but aftershocks in the M4-5 range continued for about 36 hours — normal for that fault system.

**3/5** Japan: M6.1 off the Noto Peninsula. Tsunami advisory was lifted within an hour. Our GDACS layer kept it at yellow tier throughout.

**4/5** East Africa: WHO flagged a cholera cluster in South Sudan that's moved into a second state. Case count is still under 500, so international reporting is thin, but the geographic spread is what makes this one worth tracking.

**5/5** West Africa: Mpox cases in DRC continue to trend upward. Nothing at the level of the 2024 declaration, but the signal hasn't gone back to baseline the way it usually does.

**Why it works:** Brand emoji, one region per tweet, specific magnitudes and case counts, explicit about what the WHO/GDACS layers are showing. Doesn't over-interpret. Ends on a "signal hasn't gone back to baseline" line that a smart friend would actually say.

## 5. Daily Brief: Satellite & Space Week

**1/5** 🔭 Space week on our layers — five things that moved.

**2/5** SpaceX launched another 23 Starlink satellites overnight, bringing the operational constellation to ~6,200. Our satellite layer now shows about 20% of active birds are direct-to-cell capable.

**3/5** Russia launched a Soyuz-2.1b with what appears to be a Kosmos-series payload. Our orbital layer is still resolving the final orbit — it looks like a high-inclination optical recon bird, but we'll confirm when the TLEs stabilize.

**4/5** China put a Long March 5 up carrying what they called a "technology experiment" satellite. That phrasing usually means GEO-bound, and the trajectory supports that. We'll update the layer once it finishes the transfer.

**5/5** Internet outages layer caught a Starlink degradation over Central Asia that lasted about 90 minutes. Not a full outage, and not attributed to anything on our cyber layer. Worth noting because it's the second one this week in the same region.

**Why it works:** 🔭 is the right brand emoji for space content. Each tweet pins to a specific layer. Uses appropriate hedging ("appears to be," "we'll confirm," "not attributed to anything") — the analyst voice stays disciplined even when the smart-friend voice is having fun with space stuff.
