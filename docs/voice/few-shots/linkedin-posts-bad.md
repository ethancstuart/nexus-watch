# LinkedIn Posts — Bad Examples

Ten deliberately bad LinkedIn posts. Each is annotated with the voice
violations the eval harness and human reviewer should catch.

---

## 1.

**Bad post:**

"🚀🚀🚀 SUPER excited to share that I've been building NexusWatch for the past year and we just hit a MASSIVE milestone! 🎉 I'm humbled by the journey and grateful to everyone who has supported me along the way. What do YOU think about geopolitical intelligence? Drop your thoughts below! 👇👇👇"

**Why it's bad:**
- First-person "I" and "me" throughout (hard fail — must be "we").
- "Humbled," "excited," "massive milestone" — every LinkedIn cliché in one post.
- Multiple non-brand emojis (🚀 🎉 👇).
- "What do YOU think" is the exact growth-hacker close we banned.
- No data, no number, no geography, no layer.
- Brand is the founder, not the system.

---

## 2.

**Bad post:**

"The current geopolitical landscape is experiencing heightened kinetic activity across multiple vectors. Assessment indicates continuing elevation of tension posture across the Indo-Pacific theater and MENA region. Strategic patience is advised."

**Why it's bad:**
- Pure analyst cosplay, 0% smart-friend.
- "Landscape," "kinetic," "vectors," "assessment," "posture," "theater," "strategic patience" — the whole jargon checklist.
- No specific country, no specific number, no specific layer.
- Passive voice in two out of three sentences.
- Reads like a parody of a think-tank op-ed.
- A reader learns literally nothing actionable.

## 3.

**Bad post:**

"Biden/Trump (whichever is in office) is handling the [region] situation completely wrong. What we need is a real leader who understands that [political opinion]. NexusWatch users, what do you think — is the administration asleep at the wheel?"

**Why it's bad:**
- Partisan US politics — forbidden topic.
- Names a sitting president and takes a side.
- Opinion without anchor.
- Turns the account into a political commentary account, which is not what we are.
- "NexusWatch users" in the third person is weirdly salesy.

---

## 4.

**Bad post:**

"Today I was having my morning coffee when I saw something that made my jaw drop. Three wildfires. Three. All in the same region. Let me tell you a story about what this means..."

**Why it's bad:**
- First-person "I" narrative.
- "Coffee story" opening — LinkedIn-brain cliché.
- "Made my jaw drop" — emotive adjective doing fact-work.
- "Let me tell you a story" — the least intelligence-account opening possible.
- Starts with the team member instead of the system.

---

## 5.

**Bad post:**

"🧠 THREAD: Here's everything you need to know about the Middle East right now (a MEGA BREAKDOWN you won't find anywhere else)

1/15 The situation is crazy right now 🤯
2/15 First, let me give you some background...
3/15 Actually, let me back up even further..."

**Why it's bad:**
- LinkedIn threads are not a format we use — that's an X thing. On LinkedIn we write posts, not mega-threads.
- "🧠 MEGA BREAKDOWN" is content-farm framing.
- Non-brand emoji (🧠 🤯).
- "You won't find anywhere else" is an unfalsifiable brag.
- "Let me back up even further" is padding.
- Zero data in the first three items.

---

## 6.

**Bad post:**

"Unpopular opinion: most geopolitical intelligence platforms are a scam. Here's why our competitors are wrong and we're right. [6 bullet points trashing named competitors]"

**Why it's bad:**
- Personal attack framing against competitors.
- "Unpopular opinion" is a tell.
- Zero data backing the critique.
- Positions us as the kind of account that fights on LinkedIn, which we explicitly don't do.
- Names competitors in a way that invites flame wars.

---

## 7.

**Bad post:**

"Big announcement coming tomorrow! You won't want to miss this. Can't say more yet but it's going to change EVERYTHING. Follow us to make sure you don't miss the reveal."

**Why it's bad:**
- Classic engagement-bait announcement post with no actual content.
- All-caps "EVERYTHING."
- "Can't say more yet" — the empty hype pattern.
- "Follow us" CTA is the exact thing a professional account doesn't need to beg for.
- No data, no value, just tease.

---

## 8.

**Bad post:**

"The earthquake today was absolutely devastating. Our hearts go out to everyone affected. Prayers for the region. 🙏😢"

**Why it's bad:**
- "Hearts go out" and "prayers" are condolence-post filler.
- 🙏 and 😢 are not in brand set.
- No magnitude, no location specificity, no depth, no USGS reference.
- This is the kind of post a brand does when they have nothing to say. We should have something to say — we have the data.

---

## 9.

**Bad post:**

"Did you know that the current administration has secretly been working with [adversary nation] on a classified deal that most Americans don't know about? Our analysts have uncovered evidence that suggests..."

**Why it's bad:**
- Conspiracy-adjacent framing — forbidden topic.
- "Our analysts have uncovered" overclaims what the platform does.
- "Evidence that suggests" is weasel phrasing.
- Unsourced, unfalsifiable, and the exact reputational risk the spec exists to prevent.
- Would ruin the account's credibility in one post.

---

## 10.

**Bad post:**

"At NexusWatch, we believe in leveraging synergies across our data ecosystem to deliver best-in-class actionable insights for mission-critical decision-makers navigating an increasingly volatile global landscape. Let us be your trusted partner in intelligence."

**Why it's bad:**
- Every corporate buzzword in a single sentence: "leverage," "synergies," "ecosystem," "best-in-class," "actionable insights," "mission-critical," "trusted partner."
- Zero facts, zero numbers, zero layer names.
- "Let us be your trusted partner" is B2B sales-deck copy, not a social post.
- A smart friend would never, ever say any of this out loud.
